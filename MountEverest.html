The Mount Everest

About Mount Everest

Mount Everest, also known as Sagarmatha in Nepali and Chomolungma in Tibetan, is the Earth's highest point above sea level. It is part of the Himalayas mountain range and is located in the border region between Nepal and Tibet (China).


Key facts

  • Elevation: 8,848.86 meters (29,031.7 feet)
  • First Ascent: Sir Edmund Hillary and Tenzing Norgay in 1953
  • Location: Nepal and Tibet
  • Significance: A popular destination for mountaineers and a natural wonder of the world.

Visiting Mount Everest

If you're interested in visiting Mount Everest, make sure to plan your trip carefully and be prepared for extreme conditions and altitude. The base camp area is a popular starting point for trekkers and climbers.
